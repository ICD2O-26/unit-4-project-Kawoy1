What Problem Does This Technology Solve?

For the longest time, people have been trying to design 3d things using 2d tools, and while some develop the talent and skill to do so, there will still be a gap. Engineers for example are forced to guess and estimate how a 3d object will actually fit into our three dimensional world. SensoryX AR technologies bridge this gap and allow designing and working in our 3d world. Other faults in attempts to bridge this gap include, lack of precision and non-aesthetic technology design. SensoryX creates a “Zero-Boundary” interface using everyday object forms like rings and pencils that are tracked efficiently by a computer.

How Does It Work, the Engineering Secret

While most other companies use cameras to track hands, companies like SensoryX use a fusion of different types of engineering.

  • Ultrasonic Tracking: The ring and pencil by SensoryX emit high frequencies. Microphones pick up the frequencies to figure out the position of the emitter with sub-millimiter precision.
  • Inertial Sensors or IMUs: They are tiny gyroscopes inside the ring that track the tilt and roll of your finger and relay this information to the main computer or other devices.
  • 6 Degrees of Freedom: Because of the soundwaves the system is able to triangulate the location of the emitters/devices.
    1. Up/Down
    2. Left/Right
    3. Forward/Back
    4. Pitch
    5. Yaw
    6. Roll
  • This makes precise results that don’t need a line of sight.

Where is it used today, Industry & Careers

This technology is commonly used in:

  • Aerospace & Automotive Engineering: Companies that do business in manufacturing aircraft often use tools like this to design different parts of a plane with more accuracy and detail.
  • Medical/Surgical Training: Doctors can use the Maliang Pencil to practice surgeries in VR. The precision of the tech allows them to practice incisions that require the exact hand-eye coordination.
  • Industrial Training: Workers in factories that may present hazardous circumstances use SpacialTags which are trackers on real tools. This lets them do safe repairs in a virtual environment and access all risks and dangers before proceeding with the real thing.
  • Careers: This technology opens up a whole new world of careers. This includes but is not limited to Spatial UI design, Mixed Reality Engineers, Haptic UX researchers, e.t.c.

What are its Impacts

  • Reduced Prototyping Costs: Engineers can spend less money spending real life materials on prototypes, and redesigning prototypes. Instead they can do this with tech like SensoryX, saving them time and money.
  • Accessibility: This can help people with disabilities still interact with the world and do other things because of the simple and easy usability of the technology.
  • Ergonomics This can help people who spend lots of time at a desk carry out their work in healthier ways. This is especially important for people who spend a lot of time designing things but can also be expanded to other roles.
